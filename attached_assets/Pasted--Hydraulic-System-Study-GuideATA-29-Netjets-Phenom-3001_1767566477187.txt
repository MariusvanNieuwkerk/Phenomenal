💧 Hydraulic System Study GuideATA 29 | Netjets Phenom 3001. General OverviewThe Phenom 300 uses a Constant Pressure hydraulic system. It is designed to be robust and relatively simple.Nominal Pressure: 3000 PSI.Fluid Type: Synthetic Hydrocarbon (MIL-PRF-87257).Note: This fluid is red and highly flammable.Power Source: Two Engine Driven Pumps (EDP).There is one pump per engine.If the engine is turning, the pump is supplying flow. There are no electric backup pumps.2. Key Components (The "Smart" Parts)A. The Reservoir (Manifold)This is not just a simple tank. It uses a Spring-Rolling Diaphragm.Function: It mechanically pressurizes the fluid entering the pump.Why? To prevent "foaming" and "cavitation" (air bubbles) during high-altitude flight or negative-G maneuvers.Filters: Fluid is filtered on both the pressure line (going out) and the return line (coming back).B. The AccumulatorA 25 cu.in cylinder located in the nose.Role 1: Acts as a shock absorber for pressure fluctuations.Role 2 (Emergency): Stores energy for the Emergency/Parking Brake.Capacity: In case of dual pump failure, the accumulator provides enough pressure for 6 full brake applications.C. Fire Shutoff Valves (FSOV)Located between the reservoir and the pump (suction side).Power: Powered by the Electrical Emergency Bus (Critical knowledge!).Operation:Manual: Controlled by the switches in the cockpit.Automatic: Will automatically close if High Temperature is detected in the fluid.3. Consumers (Who uses the power?)The hydraulic system powers 5 specific systems. Memorize this list:Landing GearMain Brakes (Normal & Emergency)Multi-function Spoilers (Roll control & Ground spoilers)Stick PusherRudder Spring Actuator4. System Logic: The Priority ValveWhat happens if hydraulic flow is low (e.g., engines at idle) and you demand too much power?The system has a Priority Valve.The Logic: It restricts fluid flow to the Landing Gear.The Goal: To guarantee full pressure is available for the Flight Controls (Spoilers) and Brakes.Operational Note: Your gear might retract slower, but you will always have control of the aircraft.5.
