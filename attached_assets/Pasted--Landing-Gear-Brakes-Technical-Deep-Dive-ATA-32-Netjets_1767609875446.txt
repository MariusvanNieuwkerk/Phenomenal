1. Nose Landing Gear (NLG) Mechanics
The nose gear retracts forward and includes specific mechanisms for ground handling and stowage.

Centering Unit: A cam-type centering unit inside the shock strut ensures the wheel is mechanically centered before entering the wheel well.

Shimmy Damper: Located on the upper torque link, it prevents rapid oscillation of the nose wheel on the ground.

2. Main Landing Gear (MLG) Mechanics
The MLG utilizes a Trailing Arm suspension system, providing optimized shock absorption and passenger comfort during touchdown.

Tires & Heat Protection:

Fusible Plugs: The main wheels feature thermal relief plugs. If brake heat is excessive (e.g., after a high-energy RTO), the core melts to release tire pressure safely, preventing a tire explosion in the wheel well.

Wear Pins:

Located on the brake assembly (Face A).

Check: Brake wear pins must be checked with the Parking Brake SET. If the pin is flush with the housing, the brake unit must be replaced.

Sensors:

Each MLG strut has two Proximity Switches for "Weight-on-Wheels" (WOW) logic (Total 4 sensors for redundancy).

3. Landing Gear Warning Logic (Aural)
The Landing Gear Aural Warning ("Horn") logic is governed by Altitude, Airspeed, and Flap position.

1. Cannot be Silenced:

Flap Lever: Set to 3 or FULL.

Condition: Gear is not Down & Locked.

Result: The horn sounds and the "WRN INHIB" button is disabled.

2. Can be Silenced:

Flap Lever: Set to 0, 1, or 2.

Condition:

Radio Altitude is below 700 ft AGL.

Airspeed is below 160 KIAS.

Thrust Levers: One is below 40^\circ (One Engine Inop) OR both are below 26^\circ (Two Engines).

4. Brake Control Unit (BCU) Protections
The Brake-by-Wire system (powered by the Emergency Bus) includes four specific logic protections.

A. Locked Wheel Protection
Purpose: Prevents deep tire skidding/bursting.

Logic: The BCU compares wheel speeds. If the slower wheel is ≤30% of the reference velocity (fastest wheel), a locked condition is detected.

Action: Brake pressure is fully released on the locked wheel until speed recovers.

Deactivation: This function is inhibited below 30 kts to allow for low-speed maneuvering and pivot turns.

B. Antiskid Protection
Purpose: Maintains optimal braking efficiency.

Logic: If wheel deceleration is excessive (impending skid), pressure is modulated.

Deactivation: Antiskid is inhibited below 10 kts. Below this speed, you have full manual braking power.

C. Touchdown Protection
Purpose: Prevents landing with brakes applied (blown tires).

Logic: Braking is inhibited until:

Wheel speed spins up to > 60 kts, OR

3 seconds have elapsed since Weight-on-Wheels (WOW).

Transition: After WOW, the spin-up threshold reduces linearly from 60 kts to 30 kts over the first 3 seconds.

D. Gear Retract Braking
Purpose: Stops gyroscopic forces of spinning wheels in the well.

Logic: When the gear lever is moved to UP, the BCU applies brake pressure.

Duration: Pressure is applied for a maximum of 3 seconds or until wheel speed is 0.

5. Emergency Systems
Emergency/Parking Brake

Capacity: The accumulator (separate from the main system) provides 6 full brake applications.

Pressure:

Light ON: The "PARKING BRAKE" light illuminates when pressure in the line is sufficient to hold the aircraft.

Caution: No Antiskid protection is available.

Caution: If the "EMER BRK LO PRES" CAS message is on, accumulator pressure is too low for safe parking/braking.

Emergency Extension (Free-Fall)

Actuation Force: Maximum pull force required is 25 kgf (55 lbf).

Mechanism:

Bypasses the Ldg Selector Valve (dumps pressure to return).

Mechanically releases the Uplock Boxes.

Gear extends via gravity and aerodynamic loads.

Nose gear locking is assisted by the "Downlock Springs".
