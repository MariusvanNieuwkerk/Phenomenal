⚙️ Landing Gear & Brakes: Deep DiveATA 32 | Netjets Phenom 3001. System Architecture (The Basics)The Phenom 300 features a tricycle retractable landing gear.Actuation: Hydraulically actuated (using system pressure).Command: Electrically commanded (via the cockpit lever).Holding Logic: The gear is held in the UP position by mechanical Uplock Boxes and in the DOWN position by internal mechanical locks in the actuators (Downlocks).2. The Nose Landing Gear (NLG)The nose gear is designed not just for landing, but for precise ground handling.Retraction & StructureThe NLG retracts forward into the nose fuselage.It features a Centering Unit inside the shock strut. This ensures the wheel automatically straightens when you take off, so it fits into the wheel well correctly.Shimmy Damper: A small hydraulic unit attached to the strut that prevents the nose wheel from vibrating (wobbling) rapidly during high-speed taxi or landing rolls.Steering & TowingThe Phenom 300 does not use a hydraulic steer-by-wire system. It uses a direct mechanical connection.Pedal Steering: Pushing the rudder pedals gives you $\pm 20^\circ$ of steering.Differential Braking: By applying brakes on one side and adding power, you can increase the turn radius to $\pm 43^\circ$ (Curb-to-Curb turn).Towing: The "Torque Links" (the scissors on the gear leg) can be disconnected. This allows the nose wheel to "Free Caster" 360 degrees for towing without damaging the steering mechanism.3. The Main Landing Gear (MLG)The main gear retracts inward toward the fuselage centerline.Suspension & WheelsTrailing Arm: The gear uses a "trailing arm" suspension design. This provides smoother touchdowns and better shock absorption compared to straight struts.Fusible Plugs: The wheels contain thermal relief plugs. If the brakes get too hot (e.g., after a rejected takeoff), the core of these plugs melts to release tire pressure safely, preventing a tire explosion.Wear Pins: There is a visible pin on the brake assembly. If the pin is flush with the housing, the brakes need replacing.Pilot Note: The Parking Brake must be SET to check the wear pins accurately.4. Cockpit Controls & ProtectionThe system is controlled by the LDG GEAR lever.Safety Interlock (Solenoid)There is a solenoid (electrical lock) inside the panel.Function: It physically prevents you from moving the lever to UP while the aircraft is on the ground (Weight-on-Wheels signal).Downlock Release Button: If this solenoid fails (or you need to retract immediately for an emergency), pressing the DN LCK REL button allows you to bypass the lock and move the lever.The Warning Horn (Aural Warning)The aircraft yells at you if the gear is NOT down and you are in a landing configuration.Flaps 0, 1, or 2: The horn sounds if you have low altitude (<700 ft RA) and low thrust. This can be silenced.Flaps 3 or FULL: The horn sounds regardless of thrust. This CANNOT be silenced by the mute button. This is a critical safety feature.5. The Main Brake System (Brake-by-Wire)The Phenom 300 brakes are Brake-by-Wire. There is no physical cable connecting your pedals to the brake valve. It is all electronic.ArchitectureTransducers: Sensors on the rudder pedals measure how hard you press.BCU (Brake Control Unit): The computer processes this signal.Critical Knowledge: The BCU is powered by the EMERGENCY BUS. If you lose all generators, you still have normal braking.Hydraulics: The BCU commands the valves to send 3000 PSI pressure to the carbon brakes.The 4 BCU ProtectionsThe computer provides four layers of protection:Locked Wheel Protection:Active: Above 30 kts.Logic: If one wheel is 30% slower than the other (locking up), pressure is released to let it spin up again. Prevents blown tires.Antiskid Protection:Active: Above 10 kts.Logic: Compares wheel speed to aircraft reference speed to prevent skidding. Note: Not available for the emergency brake.Touchdown Protection:Logic: Prevents you from landing with brakes applied. Braking is inhibited until wheel speed > 60 kts OR 3 seconds after Weight-on-Wheels.Gear Retract Braking:Logic: Automatically applies brakes during retraction to stop the wheels from spinning inside the wheel well.6. Emergency & Parking BrakeThis is a completely independent backup system.Actuation: A mechanical "T-Handle" in the cockpit. It pulls a physical cable connected to a specific brake valve.Hydraulics: It uses a dedicated Accumulator (separate from the main system).Capacity: The accumulator holds enough pressure for 6 full brake applications.Indication: The "PARKING BRAKE" light turns ON when pressure in the brake lines is sufficient to hold the aircraft.Note: No Antiskid protection is available when using this handle.7. Emergency Extension (Free-Fall)If the hydraulic system fails, you can extend the gear using gravity.The ProcedurePull the Free-Fall Handle: Located in the floor between the pilots.Pressure Dump: The valve opens to dump all hydraulic pressure in the gear lines back to the return line (removing the force holding the gear up).Unlock: The mechanical uplocks are released.Gravity: The gear falls down.Main Gear: Locking is assisted by the "Drag Brace" and aerodynamic drag.Nose Gear: Locking is assisted by springs and airflow.Main Gear Stuck? You may need to yaw (slip) the aircraft to use aerodynamic load to force the gear into the locked position.8. Indications & CAS MessagesMFD Symbols:🟢 Green Circle: Down & Locked.⬜ White Box: Up & Locked.🟡 Amber Hatched: In Transit.🔴 Red Icon: Disagreement (Gear is not in the selected position after 30 seconds).Key CAS Messages:LG LEVER DISAG (Warning): The gear position does not match the lever position after 30 seconds.LG WOW SYS FAIL (Caution): There is a fault in the Weight-on-Wheels sensors.BRK FAIL (Caution): Total loss of braking capability on one or both main wheels.ANTISKID FAIL (Caution): Antiskid is offline. Crucial: You must multiply your landing distance (Factor x2.something, refer to Performance charts).EMER BRK LO PRES (Caution): The accumulator for the emergency brake is low. You may not have the full 6 applications available.
