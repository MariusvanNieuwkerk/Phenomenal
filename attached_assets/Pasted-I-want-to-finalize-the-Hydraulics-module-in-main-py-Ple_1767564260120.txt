# 💧 Hydraulic System: Deep Dive
*ATA 29 - System Description & Logic*

### 1. General Architecture
The Phenom 300 hydraulic system is a "Constant Pressure" system operating at **3000 PSIG**, utilizing synthetic hydrocarbon fluid (MIL-PRF-87257).
* **Power:** Pressure is supplied by two **Engine Driven Pumps (EDP)**. As long as the engines are turning, the system is pressurized.
* **The Reservoir:** Unlike simpler aircraft, this reservoir uses a **Spring-Rolling Diaphragm**. This mechanical spring applies constant pressure to the fluid, preventing foaming and cavitation during high-altitude flight or negative-G maneuvers.

### 2. Priority & Protection
The system is designed to keep the flight controls working, even if flow is low.
* **Priority Valve:** If system flow decreases (e.g., low idle), this valve restricts fluid to the **Landing Gear** to guarantee pressure for the **Flight Controls** and **Brakes**.
* **Accumulator:** A 25 cu.in accumulator dampens pressure spikes and provides stored energy for the **Emergency/Parking Brake** (Good for 6 full actuations).

### 3. Cockpit Controls
The system is managed via the **HYD PUMP SOV** switches on the lower panel.

[INSERT IMAGE: 'hyd_panel.png']
*(Caption: Hydraulic Pump Shutoff Valves)*

* **Normal (OPEN):** The Fire Shutoff Valve (FSOV) is open.
* **CLOSED:** Electrically closes the FSOV. Powered by the **Emergency Bus**.
* **Auto-Close Logic:** The FSOV automatically closes if a **High Temperature** is detected to prevent pumping superheated fluid through the airframe.

### 4. CAS Messages & Emergency Logic
