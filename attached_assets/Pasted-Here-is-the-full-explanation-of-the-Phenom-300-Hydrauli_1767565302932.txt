💧 Hydraulic System - Study Guide
ATA 29 | Nominal Pressure: 3000 PSIG

1. The Basics (System Overview)
The Phenom 300 hydraulic system is a "Constant Pressure" system designed for simplicity and reliability.

Pressure: Operates at 3000 PSIG.

Fluid: Synthetic Hydrocarbon (MIL-PRF-87257).

Warning: This fluid is highly flammable and susceptible to contamination.

Power Source: Pressure is generated by two Engine Driven Pumps (EDP)—one on each engine.

Rule of Thumb: As long as the engines are turning, the pumps are supplying flow. There are no electric backup pumps.

2. The "Smart" Reservoir
The Phenom uses a unique reservoir design to handle high-altitude operations without needing complex bleed-air pressurization.

Spring-Rolling Diaphragm: The reservoir contains a mechanical spring that keeps the fluid under constant pressure.

Why is this important? It forces fluid into the pumps to prevent foaming and cavitation (air bubbles), even during negative-G maneuvers or rapid descents.

3. System Consumers (Who uses the power?)
The hydraulic manifold filters the fluid and distributes it to 5 specific systems:

Landing Gear

Main Brakes (Normal & Emergency)

Multi-function Spoilers (Roll control & Ground spoilers)

Stick Pusher

Rudder Spring Actuator

💡 The Priority Valve (Load Shedding) What happens if engine RPM is low (low flow) and you try to retract the gear while maneuvering?

The system features a Priority Valve.

Logic: If flow drops, the valve restricts fluid to the Landing Gear to guarantee full pressure goes to the Flight Controls and Brakes.

Result: The gear might retract slower, but you never lose control authority or braking capability.

4. Protection & Backup
FSOV (Fire Shutoff Valve):

Located between the reservoir and the pump.

Powered by the Emergency Bus (ensuring control even during electrical failure).

Auto-Close: Automatically closes if hydraulic fluid temperature becomes too high (>88°C).

Accumulator:

A 25 cu.in cylinder filled with nitrogen.

Function 1: Absorbs pressure shocks (dampener).

Function 2: Stores energy for the Emergency/Parking Brake. It provides enough pressure for 6 full brake applications if the main pumps fail.

5. Cockpit Indications
Switch Logic (HYD PUMP SOV 1 & 2):

OPEN: Normal operation.

CLOSED: Electrically closes the FSOV. Used for Engine Fire, Hydraulic High Temp, or leaks.

MFD Pressure Scale:

🟢 GREEN: > 1800 PSI (Normal).

🟡 AMBER: ≤ 1800 PSI (Caution).

6. Emergency Procedures (Killer Items)
These are the critical limitations and procedures found in the QRH.

⚠️ HYD HI TEMP (Overheat)
The fluid is too hot. The danger is not just pump failure, but a Tire Blowout. Heat transfers from the hydraulic lines into the wheel brakes, potentially fusing the tires.

The Procedure:

Close both SOVs (Isolate pumps).

Wait 7 Minutes (Cool down period).

Check if the message clears.

Logic: You open one valve at a time to identify the faulty system.

‼️ HYD LO PRES (Total Pressure Loss)
This is a critical emergency that severely affects landing performance.

What is lost?

Spoilers (expect sluggish roll rates).

Stick Pusher (no stall protection).

Anti-skid & Normal Braking.

Landing Distance Penalty: Because you lose spoilers (lift dump) and anti-skid, you must multiply your landing distance:

Dry or Wet: Factor x 2.70

Icing Conditions: Factor x 3.10 (Example: A 3,000 ft requirement becomes 9,300 ft in icing!)
